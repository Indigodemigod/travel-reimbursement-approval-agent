# Company Travel & Reimbursement Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 1 April 2026  
**Applies To:** All full-time employees, contractors on assignment, and authorized business travellers  
**Owner:** Human Resources & Finance

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the rules and limits for business-related travel expenses incurred by employees on behalf of the company. All travel must be undertaken for legitimate business purposes and expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and supported by appropriate documentation.

Employees are expected to exercise sound judgment and spend company funds as they would their own. Claims that do not comply with this policy may be partially or fully rejected.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to domestic and international business travel, including:

- Client visits and sales meetings
- Conferences, training, and industry events
- Inter-office travel between company locations
- Approved relocation or extended assignment travel

Personal extensions to business trips are not reimbursable. Any personal portion of a mixed-purpose trip must be excluded from the claim.

---

## 3. Flight Policy

### 3.1 Economy Class

Economy class airfare is reimbursable for all employees travelling on approved business trips, subject to the approval matrix in Section 8.

Employees should book flights at least **14 days in advance** where practicable. Last-minute bookings require a written justification in the expense claim.

### 3.2 Business Class

Business class airfare is **not reimbursable** for employees below the Director grade level.

Business class is permitted **only** for:

- Directors and above (Grade D1 and higher)
- Employees with a documented medical requirement approved by HR (prior written approval required)

Employees below Director grade who upgrade at their own expense will not be reimbursed for the fare difference. Claims submitted for business class without prior authorization will be rejected or downgraded to the equivalent economy fare.

### 3.3 General Flight Rules

- Only the most direct and cost-effective routing should be selected.
- Airline loyalty points earned on company-funded tickets belong to the employee and may not be used to offset company expenses.
- Cancellation or change fees are reimbursable only when caused by a business reason approved by the employee's manager.

---

## 4. Hotel Accommodation

### 4.1 Nightly Rate Limit

The maximum reimbursable hotel rate is **₹5,000 INR per night** (inclusive of taxes, excluding incidentals).

Incidentals such as minibar charges, in-room entertainment, laundry (unless on trips exceeding 5 nights), and personal purchases are not reimbursable.

### 4.2 Booking Guidelines

- Employees should use company-preferred hotel partners where available.
- Shared accommodation with a colleague of the same gender is encouraged for cost efficiency when both are travelling on the same business purpose.
- Hotel stays beyond the official trip dates require manager approval in advance.

### 4.3 Exceptions

Exceeding the nightly cap requires **prior written approval** from the employee's manager and a documented business justification (e.g., conference hotel with no alternative within policy limits, safety concerns in remote locations).

---

## 5. Meals & Daily Allowance

### 5.1 Daily Meal Limit

The maximum reimbursable amount for meals is **₹1,500 INR per day** while on approved business travel.

This limit covers breakfast, lunch, dinner, and reasonable refreshments during the travel period. Alcohol is **not reimbursable** under any circumstances.

### 5.2 Guidelines

- Itemized meal receipts are required when individual meal expenses exceed ₹500 INR (see Section 7).
- Team meals may be claimed by the senior-most attendee; duplicate claims for the same meal are prohibited.
- Per-diem meal claims without receipts are permitted only up to ₹500 INR per day and require manager acknowledgment.

---

## 6. Local Transport

### 6.1 Reimbursable Modes

The following local transport expenses are reimbursable when incurred for business purposes during a trip:

- **Taxi** (including app-based cab services such as Uber, Ola, and equivalent providers)
- **Metro** and other approved public transit (bus, local train) where safe and practical

Receipts or digital trip summaries are required for taxi claims above ₹500 INR.

### 6.2 Non-Reimbursable Transport

The following are **not reimbursable**:

- **Personal vehicle fuel** and mileage for privately owned cars, motorcycles, or two-wheelers
- Parking fines, traffic violations, and toll charges on personal vehicles (unless pre-approved for a company vehicle)
- Chauffeur-driven private car hire without prior finance approval
- Commuting between home and the employee's regular office location

Employees requiring a rental car for business use must obtain **prior manager approval** and book through the company travel desk where available.

---

## 7. Receipts & Documentation

### 7.1 Receipt Requirement

**Every expense above ₹500 INR requires a valid receipt** or equivalent digital proof of payment.

Acceptable documentation includes:

- Tax invoices and GST-compliant receipts
- E-tickets and boarding passes for flights
- Hotel folios showing nightly rate and dates
- App-generated trip receipts for cab services
- Credit/debit card statements **only when** paired with the underlying itemized receipt

Handwritten notes, pro-forma invoices, and unsigned estimates are not accepted.

### 7.2 Claim Submission

- Expense claims must be submitted within **30 calendar days** of trip completion.
- Claims must include date, vendor name, amount, currency, business purpose, and supporting receipt for each line item.
- Expenses submitted after 30 days require manager and finance approval and may be rejected.

### 7.3 Missing Receipts

Expenses without required receipts will not be reimbursed unless escalated under Section 9 (Manual Review Cases).

---

## 8. Approval Matrix

Reimbursement claims are routed based on the **total claim amount** (sum of all approved line items):

| Total Claim Amount (INR) | Approval Authority        | Processing |
|--------------------------|---------------------------|------------|
| Below ₹10,000            | Auto-approval             | System may approve eligible claims that pass all policy checks |
| ₹10,000 – ₹50,000        | Manager approval          | Direct manager must review and approve |
| Above ₹50,000            | Finance approval          | Finance team must review and approve |

Auto-approval applies only when the claim:

- Is fully compliant with this policy
- Includes all required receipts
- Contains no flagged items under Section 9

Managers and Finance may reject, partially approve, or request additional documentation at their discretion.

---

## 9. Manual Review Cases

The following situations **require manual review** and are not eligible for auto-approval, regardless of claim amount:

1. **Missing receipts** — Any line item above ₹500 INR without acceptable documentation
2. **Unsupported categories** — Expenses outside approved categories (e.g., personal shopping, entertainment, gifts without prior approval)
3. **Foreign currency** — Claims with expenses denominated in currencies other than INR (conversion to INR at the company monthly exchange rate applies; original receipts required)
4. **Policy conflict** — Any expense that appears to violate one or more sections of this policy, including:
   - Business class travel below Director grade
   - Hotel rates exceeding ₹5,000 per night without approval
   - Meal claims exceeding ₹1,500 per day
   - Personal vehicle fuel claims
   - Duplicate or overlapping expense entries

Manual review cases are routed to the appropriate approver per Section 8 and may result in partial reimbursement, full rejection, or a request for clarification.

---

## 10. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are never reimbursable:

- Personal travel, sightseeing, and leisure activities
- Spouse or dependent travel unless pre-approved by HR for relocation
- Traffic fines, visa fees for personal holidays, and travel insurance for personal trips
- Office supplies available at the employee's home location
- Tips exceeding 10% of the billed amount

---

## 11. Compliance & Audit

Finance reserves the right to audit any claim within **12 months** of reimbursement. Employees found to have submitted fraudulent or knowingly non-compliant claims may face disciplinary action, including recovery of funds and termination of employment.

Questions regarding this policy should be directed to **HR Operations** or **Accounts Payable**.
